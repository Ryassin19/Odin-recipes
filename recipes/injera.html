Description

Injera is a traditional Ethiopian and Eritrean flatbread made from teff flour, a tiny, nutrient-rich grain native to the Horn of Africa. It has a soft, spongy texture and a slightly sour taste, the result of a natural fermentation process that lasts several days. The batter is poured onto a hot, flat pan and cooked on one side, creating a surface covered in tiny holes, perfect for soaking up sauces and stews.

Injera is more than just breadâ€”it also serves as a utensil. Large rounds of injera are typically spread out on a platter and topped with various dishes like lentils, vegetables, and spiced meats. Pieces are torn off by hand and used to scoop up the food. Its unique texture and tangy flavor make injera a central and symbolic part of East African meals and culture.

Ingredients

  • Teff flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Starter (fermented batter or yeast)
  • Oil (for pan prep)
  • Optional wheat flour (for texture balance)

Steps

  1. Mix teff flour with water and let it ferment for 2/3 days
  2. Stir in salt once fermentation is complete
  3. Heat a flat non-stick or clay pan
  4. Pour batter in a circular motion to cover the pan
  5. Cover and steam-cook until bubbles form and the top is set
  6. Remove gently, cool, and serve with stews or salads
